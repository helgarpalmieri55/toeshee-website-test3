# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two equal audiences:

1. **Crypto / Web3 operators** — founders, ops leads, and heads of support at exchanges, DeFi protocols, wallets, iGaming platforms. They arrive to evaluate whether Toeshee understands their world and can be trusted with their customers.
2. **Enterprise fintech buyers** — procurement and operations leaders at regulated fintech companies evaluating BPO providers. They arrive looking for credibility, track record, and clarity on scope.

Both audiences are evaluating: "do these people actually understand the complexity of my industry, and are they the best at it?"

## Product Purpose

Toeshee is the first outsourcing provider built for the decentralized economy. It provides crypto-native customer support infrastructure — covering Web3, blockchain, fintech, and iGaming — as a managed outsourced service. Success looks like a qualified buyer reaching out within minutes of landing on the site, convinced that Toeshee is the clear category leader.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Credible. Unapologetic.

The brand is premium and considered — not loud, not soft. It carries the quiet confidence of a firm that has done this before and knows things most competitors don't. Think of a boutique institutional partner, not a vendor. The orange-red accent is a deliberate edge: controlled intensity, not hype.

## Anti-references

- **AI startup clichés**: cream backgrounds, gradient text on headings, identical card grids, small all-caps eyebrow labels on every section, numbered section markers as default scaffolding.
- Avoid anything that reads as generic SaaS, loud crypto/NFT aesthetic, or old-school BPO (headsets, corporate blue).

## Design Principles

1. **Earned authority** — every visual and copy choice should signal deep expertise, not loud claims. Show proof; don't assert credibility.
2. **Controlled intensity** — the accent carries energy; the rest of the palette is restrained. Never let the page feel frenetic.
3. **Clarity over decoration** — if a design element doesn't help a buyer understand the product faster or trust it more, remove it.
4. **Category-own, don't category-follow** — Toeshee is the first of its kind; the design should feel like a reference point, not a follower of Stripe or Linear.
5. **Substance in the details** — premium is felt in spacing, contrast, type hierarchy, and motion timing — not in gradients or glass cards.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort, no formal compliance target. Minimum bar: body text contrast ≥4.5:1, keyboard navigable, reduced-motion support for all animations.
